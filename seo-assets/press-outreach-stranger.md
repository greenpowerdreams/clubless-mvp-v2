## Email Body

Hi [Editor name],

My name's Drew Green. I'm a Seattle founder and I just launched Clubless Collective — a free platform that helps Seattle DJs, promoters, and creators host profitable nightlife events without owning (or renting from) a traditional venue.

I'm writing because I think there's a story here that matters to your readers: the economics of throwing a show in Seattle have gotten brutal. Venues take 20% of the bar plus a room fee. Ticketing platforms take another 10-20%. By the time the DJ, the photographer, and security are paid, a lot of hosts walk away with less than their bartender.

Clubless tries to fix that. It bundles:

- Ticketing at 8-10% (lower than Posh, Eventbrite, Dice)
- Licensed mobile bar service (WSLCB-compliant, we bring it to any space)
- A vendor marketplace (DJs, photographers, rentals)
- A curated Seattle event calendar
- A free profit calculator so hosts can model a night before they commit

It's live at clublesscollective.com and it's already being used by a handful of local collectives I've worked with on soft launch.

I think this could make a good story for The Stranger because:

1. It's genuinely local — built here, by one person, for Seattle creators
2. It's pushing back on extraction by the big platforms (Posh, Eventbrite, Dice)
3. It ties into the broader pattern of Seattle's best events happening in warehouses, rooftops, and non-traditional rooms instead of legacy clubs

I'd love to chat — even 15 minutes on the phone — and can get you in touch with 2-3 Seattle DJs or promoters who've used it if you want quotes from the community, not just the founder.

Thanks for your time.
