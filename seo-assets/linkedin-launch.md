## Post

Today I'm launching Clubless Collective.

It's an event operating system for creators — DJs, promoters, and hosts who want to throw profitable nightlife events without owning a venue.

Here's why I built it:

Over the last two years I watched friends in Seattle's music scene throw incredible events and walk away with almost nothing. Venues were taking 20% of the bar plus a room fee. Ticketing platforms were taking another 10-20%. By the time everyone who actually built the night was paid, the host was making less than the person pouring drinks.

Meanwhile, the best events were increasingly happening outside traditional clubs — in warehouses, rooftops, lofts, galleries. The audience had moved. But the tools for creators hadn't caught up.

So I spent four months (solo, nights and weekends) building Clubless.

It bundles:
→ Ticketing at 8-10% (vs 10-20% on legacy platforms)
→ A licensed, WSLCB-compliant mobile bar service we run ourselves
→ A vendor marketplace for DJs, photographers, and event services
→ A free profit calculator so creators can model a night before committing
→ A curated Seattle event calendar
→ QR ticketing and instant Stripe payouts

Three things I learned building this:

1. Ship the landing page first, not last. I waited too long to put a public face on this and paid for it in lost inbound leads.

2. Cut scope ruthlessly. I cut 60% of my original feature list and still cut more at the end. The version that ships is always the one that was small enough to finish.

3. Bundle a physical service with your software if you want a real moat. Ticketing platforms are a commodity. A licensed mobile bar bundled with the platform is not.

If you're a creator, a venue operator, a DJ, or anyone who works in or loves Seattle's nightlife — I'd love your feedback.
