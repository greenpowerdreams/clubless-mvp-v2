## Tweet 1 (the hook)

After 4 months of solo building, Clubless Collective is live today.

Built in Seattle. For Seattle.

## Tweet 2 (the why)

Why?

Because the traditional nightclub is a bad deal for everyone except the owner.

Venues take 20% of the bar + a room fee.
Ticketing platforms take another 10-20%.
By the time the DJ + photographer + security get paid, the host walks away with less than the bartender.

## Tweet 3 (the insight)

Meanwhile, the best events in Seattle right now aren't in traditional clubs.

They're in warehouses. Rooftops. Lofts. Galleries after hours. Cafes turned dance floors at midnight.

The audience moved.

The tools haven't.

## Tweet 4 (the product)

Clubless is what happens when you unbundle the nightclub into software:

→ Ticketing at 8-10%
→ Licensed mobile bar (we bring it to any space)
→ Vendor marketplace: DJs, photographers, rentals
→ Free profit calculator
→ Curated Seattle event calendar
→ QR check-in + instant payouts

## Tweet 5 (the stack, for the nerds)

For the builders:

- React 18 + Vite + TypeScript
- Supabase (Postgres + RLS + Edge Functions)
- Stripe Connect
- Tailwind + shadcn
- Deployed on Vercel
- ~22k LOC, 38 pages
- Solo, 4 months

## Tweet 6 (the moat)

The moat isn't the software.

It's that we own the licensed mobile bar directly — WSLCB-compliant, insured, operated by us.

Any platform can do ticketing.
Bundling it with a physical bar service is what lets creators run an event end-to-end.

## Tweet 7 (the lesson)

Biggest lesson from 4 months of shipping:

Cut 60% of your scope. Then cut another 20%.

I cut a referral system, SMS marketing, wallet passes, a mobile app, and AI recommendations.

I kept: ticketing, bar, vendors, calculator, calendar, check-in.

Still cut more at the end.
