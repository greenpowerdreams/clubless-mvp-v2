The new Seattle nightlife platform for people who don't want to go to a club

## Email Body

Hi [Editor name],

I'm Drew Green, founder of Clubless Collective — a Seattle-built platform that helps local DJs, promoters, and creators host events without owning a venue. It's live today at clublesscollective.com.

I think there's a Seattle Met story here that's less about the tech and more about how Seattle's nightlife is changing:

The best small-room events in this city are increasingly happening outside the traditional club circuit — warehouses in SoDo, lofts on Capitol Hill, rooftop pop-ups, galleries after hours. The audience has moved. But the tools for creators haven't caught up: they still have to cobble together ticketing, bar licensing, vendor booking, and promotion from five different services.

Clubless bundles all of that:

- Curated Seattle event calendar (genuinely comprehensive — we're cross-listing small-room events that don't show up anywhere else)
- Licensed mobile bar service (WSLCB-compliant)
- Vendor marketplace for DJs, photographers, rentals
- Ticketing at 8-10%
- Free profit calculator

1. **Where Seattle nightlife is actually happening in 2026** — with real examples and the collectives behind them
2. **The economics of being a creator** — why talented local DJs are quietly burning out and what could fix it
3. **The rise of "unbundled" nightlife** — and why it might be the future

Happy to send more info, set up a call, or connect you with Seattle DJs and event hosts who would make great quote sources.
